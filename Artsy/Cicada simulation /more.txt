Built from the concept presented in "Murmur of many voices" and "cicada-nine".

Core idea:
- Inspired by cicada self-organizing choruses and spatial synchrony in nature.
- Participants become a decentralized sonic organism through mutual attunement.
- The work explores collective resonance as a negotiation of rhythm between human, animal, and technological bodies.

Interaction model:
- Audience joins via web app (prototype: cicada-nine.vercel.app).
- Tap/hold on mobile triggers cicada-signing simulation and frequency cycles.
- Screen flicker is synchronized to sound amplitude for visual feedback.

Exhibition setup notes:
- Designed for offline participatory sessions up to ~30 participants.
- Dimmed light environment recommended for immersion.
- Participants' phones form a multichannel sound-space.
- Includes a "cicada trainer" onboarding moment before the main experience.
