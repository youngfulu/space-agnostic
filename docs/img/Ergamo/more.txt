Ergamo is a short animated film about a middle-aged woman who becomes lost in her own memories while driving somewhere in abstract Middle East country. The intimate act of revisiting her past — quoting fragments of all the lives she has lived — gradually turns into a personal limbo, a space where the boundaries between the present moment and what has already passed begin to dissolve. The story explores multiple dimensions of memory distortion, from the deeply personal to the sociopolitical — including collective amnesia and historical erasure. By interweaving factual research with poetic speculation, Ergamo seeks to blur the line between what is remembered and what is imagined, between reality and the ghostly terrain of recollection.
