Thresholds explores the phenomenon of liminality from different perspectives — as a physical space, psychological state and rite of passage. By warping the gap between physical and digital realms, the installation merges spaces within the former Peter Baan Centre, composing an in-between state of past and present. Within the limbo, the manmade structures are no longer a psychiatric clinic not an art space, becoming a place of pure liminality, endowed with duality and very personal experience of being stuck in between of states, of being in the constant transition.



A one-month residency was held in a building formerly occupied by the Pieter Baan Centrum, a forensic psychiatric observation clinic. The abandoned and empty hallways, carrying the silent memories of people who once struggled with severe mental health issues, evoked uncanny, hallucinatory imagery. This project explores liminality as an in-between state — both physical and metaphorical. It was presented as a large-scale, site-specific multimedia installation.
